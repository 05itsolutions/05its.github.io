Orthopedics

Department of Orthopaedics at Preethi Hospitals was established in 2006 as a humble unit consists of 3 consultants which grew over a period of 12 years with 14 consultants as Preethi Institute Of Medical Sciences and Research(PIMS).

The Department is headed by Prof.Dr.M.Chidambaram, along with Prof.Dr.R.Sivakumar and Prof.Dr.M.Gulam Mohideen.

The Department has 3 units providing treatment and care in all the superspecialities of Orthopaedics care.The Department has Sub Specialities like Arthroscopy,Spine Surgery,Joint replacement surgery,deformity correction,Pediatric Orthopaedics and Hand reconstructive surgery,which is manned by speciality Concerned surgeons.

The Ortho Department in Preethi Institute of Medical Sciences and Research is a busy department around 28,000 outpatient visits with 2400 Surgeries performed per year.

We have dedicated and well equipped Orthopaedic surgery complexes. We are also one of the hospital having specialized Pediatric Orthopaedics units offering the whole range of Pediatric solutions. Our Physiotherapy units provide rehabilitation, pain management and use sophisticated equipments with protocol based therapy to provide recovery in the quickest timeframe.

The Orthopedics Department is at the forefront in offering the latest in Orthopaedic management and surgical advancements in India and across world. Our Orthopedics team comprises of renowned Doctors, Surgeons, Nurses, Physiotherapists and Rehabilitative specialists who work together to provide the best treatment for all problems of the following.

  • Joints
  • Hands
  • Shoulder
  • Elbow
  • Knee
  • Hip
  • Feet
  • Ankle
  • Cartilage
  • Tendon and much more.

The Department of Orthopedics at Preethi Hospitals provides comprehensive care for all musculo-skeletal problems through its state-of-the-art facilities at Madurai. It is an integrated centre for different specialties like Trauma / Arthroplasty / Arthroscopy / Spine Surgery and Paediatric orthopedics. Surgeons at the department, with diverse expertise, are committed to provide effective solutions to the society for a range of orthopedic problems. Minimally invasive and key-hole procedures are used whenever possible to reduce pain and ensure a speedy recovery.
